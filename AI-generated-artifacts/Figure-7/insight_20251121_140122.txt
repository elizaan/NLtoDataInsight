In our analysis of the Agulhas Current over a 14-month period, we aimed to understand the temperature variations and the dynamics of current retroreflection in this critical oceanic region. This spatial scope was chosen to capture the core areas of the Agulhas Current, where retroflection phenomena are known to occur. The temporal analysis spanned 14 months, starting from January 2020, and we sampled the data every 30 days to balance the need for temporal resolution with the constraints of data processing speed and complexity.

The decision to sample every 30 days was a tradeoff between resolution and computational efficiency. Given the dataset's size and the user’s time constraints, this approach allowed us to capture significant trends without overwhelming processing capabilities.
